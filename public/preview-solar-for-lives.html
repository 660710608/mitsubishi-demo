Mitsubishi Motors Thailand Hands Over Solar Cell System to Long Hospital in Phrae Province Under the 'Solar for Lives' Project, Reinforcing Its Commitment to Carbon Neutrality

news_featured_image

Representatives from Mitsubishi Motors Thailand, Long Hospital, EGAT, and local dealership partners at the solar system handover ceremony, Phrae Province, February 27, 2026.

news_body (rich text)

Mitsubishi Motors (Thailand) Co., Ltd. (MMTh) held the handover ceremony for solar cell systems under the "Solar for Lives" project at Long Hospital, Phrae Province, on February 27, 2026. The 50-kilowatt solar power system, valued at 1,353,550 baht, marks the 13th hospital supported under the company's 10-year mission to install solar systems in 40 community hospitals across Thailand. Total installed capacity has now exceeded 650 kilowatts nationwide.

The ceremony was presided over by Mr. Sarote Maartlert, Executive Vice President of Mitsubishi Motors Thailand, Ms. Prom-Usa Salee, and Dr. Warawut Somboon, Director of Long Hospital, along with representatives from EGAT, Thailand Greenhouse Gas Management Organization, and the local Mitsubishi dealership.

"By transforming 'energy costs' into 'medical opportunities,' hospitals can reallocate their budgets toward better patient care. Long Hospital is expected to reduce electricity expenses by up to 300,000 baht per year."

Mr. Sarote Maartlert — Executive Vice President, Mitsubishi Motors Thailand

"We are deeply grateful to Mitsubishi Motors Thailand for this generous support. The solar system will significantly reduce our operational costs, allowing us to invest more resources directly into patient care and hospital services."

Dr. Warawut Somboon — Director, Long Hospital, Phrae Province

In addition to the handover ceremony, the event featured test-drive opportunities for Mitsubishi's latest hybrid models — the ALL-NEW MITSUBISHI XFORCE HEV, MITSUBISHI XPANDER CROSS HEV, and MITSUBISHI TRITON MEGA CAB — reinforcing the company's commitment to sustainable mobility alongside its community health initiative.

The "Solar for Lives" project is part of Mitsubishi Motors Thailand's broader CSR strategy spanning the pillars of Education, Environment, and Health. Key partners include the Ministry of Public Health, EGAT, and the Thailand Greenhouse Gas Management Organization.

About Mitsubishi Motors Thailand (MMTh)

Mitsubishi Motors (Thailand) Co., Ltd. (MMTh) is a subsidiary of Mitsubishi Motors Corporation, Japan, and one of Thailand's leading automotive manufacturers. Established in 1995, MMTh produces vehicles at its Laem Chabang, Chonburi facility for both the domestic market and global export. As of 2024, cumulative production has surpassed seven million units.

MMTh's current vehicle lineup includes the Triton, Pajero Sport, Outlander, Xpander, Xpander Cross, and Xforce. The company also operates the Mitsubishi Motors Education Academy and a proving ground in Si Racha, Chonburi.
