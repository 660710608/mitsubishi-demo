Bangkok — February 27, 2026

Mitsubishi Motors Thailand Hands Over Solar Cell System to Long Hospital in Phrae Province Under the 'Solar for Lives' Project, Reinforcing Its Commitment to Carbon Neutrality

news_featured_image
[ Featured Image — Ceremony photo ]

Mr. Sarote Maartlert (EVP) and Dr. Warawut Somboon (Long Hospital Director) at the solar system handover ceremony, Phrae Province.

news_body (rich text)

Mitsubishi Motors Thailand (MMTh) handed over a 50-kilowatt solar power system valued at 1,353,550 baht to Long Hospital in Phrae Province, marking the 13th hospital supported under the company's 10-year mission to install solar cell systems in 40 community hospitals across Thailand. The total installed capacity has now exceeded 650 kilowatts nationwide.

"By transforming 'energy costs' into 'medical opportunities,' hospitals can reallocate their budgets toward better patient care. Long Hospital is expected to reduce electricity expenses by up to 300,000 baht per year."

Mr. Sarote Maartlert — Executive Vice President, Mitsubishi Motors Thailand

"We are deeply grateful to Mitsubishi Motors Thailand for this generous support. The solar system will significantly reduce our operational costs, allowing us to invest more resources directly into patient care."

Dr. Warawut Somboon — Director, Long Hospital

The ceremony also featured test-drive opportunities for Mitsubishi's latest hybrid models: the ALL-NEW MITSUBISHI XFORCE HEV, MITSUBISHI XPANDER CROSS HEV, and MITSUBISHI TRITON MEGA CAB, reinforcing the company's commitment to sustainable mobility alongside the solar handover event.

About Mitsubishi Motors Thailand (MMTh)

Mitsubishi Motors (Thailand) Co., Ltd. (MMTh) is a subsidiary of Mitsubishi Motors Corporation, Japan. Established in 1995, MMTh manufactures and sells vehicles in Thailand and exports to global markets. As of 2024, cumulative production has exceeded seven million units.

MMTh operates a production facility in Laem Chabang, Chonburi, along with a Mitsubishi Motors Education Academy and a proving ground in Si Racha. The company's vehicle lineup includes the Triton, Pajero Sport, Outlander, Xpander, and Xforce models.
