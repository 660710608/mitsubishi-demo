Mitsubishi Motors Thailand Hands Over Solar Cell System to Long Hospital in Phrae Province Under the 'Solar for Lives' Project

Reinforcing commitment to carbon neutrality — 50 kW solar system valued at 1,353,550 baht, the 13th hospital supported under the company's 10-year mission.

[ Featured Image — Group ceremony photo at Long Hospital ]
news_article_content

Mitsubishi Motors Thailand (MMTh) handed over a 50-kilowatt solar power system valued at 1,353,550 baht to Long Hospital in Phrae Province, marking the 13th hospital supported under the company's 10-year mission to install solar cell systems in 40 community hospitals across Thailand. The total installed capacity has now exceeded 650 kilowatts nationwide.

news_article_quote
"By transforming 'energy costs' into 'medical opportunities,' hospitals can reallocate their budgets toward better patient care. Long Hospital is expected to reduce electricity expenses by up to 300,000 baht per year."
Mr. Sarote Maartlert Executive Vice President, Mitsubishi Motors Thailand
news_article_content

The ceremony also featured test-drive opportunities for three of Mitsubishi's latest hybrid models, bringing sustainable mobility directly to the local community and reinforcing the company's commitment to both environmental responsibility and community well-being.

news_article_quote
"We are deeply grateful to Mitsubishi Motors Thailand for this generous support. The solar system will significantly reduce our operational costs, allowing us to invest more resources directly into patient care and hospital services."
Dr. Warawut Somboon Director, Long Hospital, Phrae Province
